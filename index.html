Symbol

When taken in isolation, the 'M' within the logotype form our symbol. It act as a representation of the Complitech brand in its most reduced and simplified form. The symbol can be used as an endorsing mark or as a sign-off but should only be used in when the Complitech context is already evident.

In isolation it evokes confidence and authority, but it must be clear that Complitech is the sender. The symbol must never be altered, colored or combined with any other names or elements. The original symbol must be used at all times. Never modify the symbol or create a new one.

Clearspace

The Complitech logotype is most powerful when surrounded by an area of clearspace. This guarantees a high degree of visibility and legibility, while preserving its integrity.

This measurement is the minimum space allowed around the logotype and should be applied in all instances. It is important to point out that the defined clearspace is a minimum. It is of course allowed, and in some cases recommended, to go above this in order to create clear and consistent designs.

Position

In order to minimize variation and ensure a coordinated, uniform appearance, try to use the same position and size across similar applications.

The preferred positions of the Complitech logotype are shown below.

Primary position

The corners are our primary positions for the logotype. This is true for most of our formats, such as portrait, landscape and square formats.

Secondary position

In some instances, due to content and format, the logotype can be placed in secondary position. Occasionally, in branding, a centered logotype is preferred, but all of the positions shown here are available for use.

Background and text

Our background colors are almost always the C White, C Black, C Red or C Grey. Use our secondary colors and our information color palette as background colors. Our main text colors are C White, C Black, C Grey and C Red. White is used on dark background and the C Black on light backgrounds.


Be economical in the use of color. The palette contains bold colors, and as such, itâ€™s important to be considered and restrained in their use.

Always ensure that the pairing of colors achieves adequate contrast for maximum visibility and legibility.

In infographics and other supporting elements our information colors can be used. However, they can never be used on main messages or as a brand carrier.
